This marks the first successful bring-up of the embedded execution layer.

---

### 2. B Channel Debugging and Pin Mapping Issues Resolved

A large part of today's work focused on debugging the B motor channel.

During this process, I identified several important engineering issues:

- MCU pin name is not equal to board silk label
- PB0 corresponds to **A3**
- PB1 is not available on the normal Arduino-style pin row
- PB5 was later selected as a better replacement for BIN2
- wrong pin assumptions can directly cause abnormal motor behavior

This debugging process helped clarify a very important embedded principle:

> software pin naming, board header mapping, and actual wiring location must be treated separately

---

### 3. Motor Direction and Turning Behavior Adjusted

After the dual motor system was brought up, I further adjusted:

- motor direction definitions
- left/right wheel motion logic
- turning behavior
- speed constants

Key findings:

- too small duty values may not overcome motor start threshold
- turning is not just “one wheel slow” in theory, but also depends on whether the wheel can physically start under load
- B motor forward/reverse logic may need to be inverted depending on actual wiring and wheel orientation

This moved the project from “it can move” toward “it can move correctly”.

---

### 4. Control Layer Introduced

Today was not only about motion bring-up.

I also began moving from:

**fixed autonomous motion sequence**

to:

**external command-based control**

A serial-control structure was introduced conceptually and then implemented in software, with command mapping such as:

- `w` -> forward
- `s` -> backward
- `a` -> left
- `d` -> right
- `x` -> stop

This marks the beginning of the **control layer** above the execution layer.

---

### 5. Power and System Stability Issues Exposed

Today’s debugging also exposed an important reality of real embedded systems:

many failures were not caused by logic errors, but by physical-layer problems such as:

- loose battery contacts
- unstable Dupont wiring
- poor pin mapping assumptions
- weak startup torque at low duty

This is actually a major project milestone, because it means the work has moved beyond “code only” and into **real hardware-system debugging**.
